Piloting Safety Net "Transfer Modalities Research Initiative" Pilot Survey, 2012-2013, Commissioned by IFPRI and funded by USAID

Survey Type: Endline    Survey Tag: Health,Enducation    Last Updated: 02/02/2017 

The World Food Programme (WFP) and IFPRI in Bangladesh is pilot testing alternative transfer modalities: food, cash, food-vouchers, and nutrition BCC. To understand the possible impacts on beneficiaries, we would evaluate a pilot intervention with 5 arms: (a) only cash; (b) only food; (c) only food voucher; (d) food + cash; and (e) nutrition BCC + cash. WFP is implementing the pilot test, and IFPRI is evaluating it. DATA is working under collaborative agreement for technical assistance and survey services.

The overall objective of implementing and evaluating the pilot test is to strengthen and streamline the social safety net system in Bangladesh by identifying and scaling up the most cost-effective, multi-year safety net program to improve the food security and livelihoods of the ultra poor. The specific objectives of evaluation are to:

1. Establish relevance of the transfer modalities in addressing poverty, food insecurity and undernutrition of the ultra poor households.
2. Document, using rigorous impact evaluation designs, the impact and cost-effectiveness of each of the 5 arms of the pilot test on key outcomes.
